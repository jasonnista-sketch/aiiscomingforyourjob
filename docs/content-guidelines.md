- **Specific over vague.** "AI can draft a 40-page contract in minutes" beats "AI is transforming the legal industry."

## Scoring Criteria

### Risk Score (0-100)
"How likely AI is to fully automate core tasks in this job within 5 years."

Base your score on:
- What % of daily tasks can current AI tools already do?
- How physical/interpersonal is the work? (physical = lower risk)
- How regulated is the field? (regulation = slower adoption)
- Are there already production-ready AI tools displacing this work?

Calibration anchors:
- 8% = Electrician (very physical, hard to automate)
- 15% = Registered Nurse (human connection core, AI assists only)
- 35% = Attorney (research/drafting automated, judgment/advocacy not)
- 52% = Paralegal (document review heavily automated)
- 58% = Radiologist (AI reads images, but oversight still needed)
- 82% = Bookkeeper (almost fully automatable)

### Augmentation Score (0-100)
"How much you can level up by learning the AI tools and skills below."

This is the OPPORTUNITY score. High augmentation means:
- Lots of AI tools exist for this role
- Learning them creates measurable competitive advantage
- Early adopters are already pulling ahead

A job can have HIGH risk AND high augmentation (Radiologist: 58% risk, 95% augment).
A job can have LOW risk AND low augmentation (Electrician: 8% risk, 45% augment).

## Writing Each Section

### Summary (2-3 sentences)
Open with the verdict â€” is this job in trouble, evolving, or safe? Then explain why. Be specific about WHAT AI is doing to this role.

### Key Insight (1-2 sentences)

Good: "Pharmacy robots can fill 300 prescriptions per hour with zero errors. Pharmacists who still just count pills are in trouble â€” those who counsel patients are not."

### Tools (3-4 per job)
- Must be REAL tools with working URLs
- Prioritize tools specific to this profession over generic AI (ChatGPT is not a "pharmacist tool")
- Include a one-line description of what it does in THIS role's context
- Prefer established products over vaporware

### Skills (4-6 per job)

## BLS Stats
Source all employment data from:

Format:

- Jobs: "322,200" (with comma)  
- Growth: "+5%" or "-2%" (with sign)
- Growth label: "Growing" (positive), "Stable" (0-1%), "Declining" (negative)
